The Craftsman

My Name Is Roberto. I Build Doors.

I've been working with wood for over 20 years. Master Door and Crown is not a company — it's me, my shop, and the doors I build from scratch for homeowners throughout Ventura County.

The Story

Twenty Years, One Door at a Time

I started in construction out of necessity, like a lot of guys in this trade. Framing, rough carpentry, whatever came through. But somewhere along the way I got put on a door crew, and that was it. There's something about a door — the way it has to be perfectly plumb, the way the grain has to run right for it to hold up over time, the way a good one feels when it swings. I never left that part of the trade.

I started noticing a pattern. Homeowners would hire someone to install a new front door. Six months later they'd call back because it was swelling, or the finish was peeling, or it never fit right to begin with. Pre-made doors are built to a standard size, finished in a warehouse, shipped across the country. They're not built for your house. They're not built for your climate. I got tired of watching that happen, so I started building them myself — from raw lumber, sized to the actual opening, finished for the actual exposure.

Wood is an honest material. It tells you when it's stressed. It shows you when the grain wasn't selected right. It reminds you every time you rush. I work mostly in oak, mahogany, walnut, and teak — each species has its own personality, its own best uses. A teak door on a coastal property in Oxnard will outlast anything else in that salt air. A walnut door on a well-protected entry in Ojai will get more beautiful with every passing year. I pick the species for the door, not the other way around.

When you call me, you get me — not a salesperson, not a project manager. I come to your house, look at the opening, look at the surrounding architecture, and we talk through what makes sense. I give you a clear number before any lumber is cut. After that, I stay in contact at every milestone — wood ordered, door in the shop, install date confirmed. I don't disappear on a project. This is a small operation by design.

I'm not trying to build a company with a dozen crews. I'm trying to build doors that last generations — the kind that get passed down with the house, that a kid grows up touching every day, that a grandkid refinishes someday and realizes what a well-built thing actually feels like. That's a long-horizon kind of work. It means I can only take on so many projects, which means every one I take matters. That's the whole point of Master Door and Crown.

Philosophy

Why Wood. Why Custom. Why One Craftsman.

These aren't just talking points. They're the reasons every decision in this business is made the way it is.

🌲

Why Wood

Wood is the only material that ages honestly. It takes a finish better, holds a repair better, and looks better at 30 years than it did at 3. Every fiberglass and steel door I've seen starts to look its age fast. A well-built solid wood door just keeps getting better — and when it needs attention, you can actually fix it.

📐

Why Custom

Your opening is what it is. Your house has proportions that exist for a reason. A door pulled off a shelf doesn't know any of that — it gets shimmed in and gapped out and called done. Custom means I measure your actual framing, build to those exact dimensions, and the door belongs there because it was made for it.

🤝

Why One Craftsman

When five different people touch a job, nobody owns the outcome. I measure it, build it, finish it, and hang it. If something's off, I know exactly where it came from — because I was there at every step. That accountability is built into how I run this business. It's also why my clients refer their neighbors to me.

How It Works

The Build Process

From the first call to the last screw, here's how every project at Master Door and Crown unfolds.

01

Inquiry & Consultation

We start with a conversation — phone or email — to understand your project. I'll ask about the door type, your home's style, your budget range, and your timeline. If it sounds like a good fit, we schedule an in-home visit.

02

Design & Measurement

I come to your home, take precise measurements of the rough opening, and discuss design options in context. I can show material samples and hardware options on-site. You'll leave the meeting knowing exactly what we're building and why.

03

Fabrication

Work begins in my shop. I select and mill the lumber, cut all joinery by hand, and assemble the door with waterproof glue and mechanical fasteners. For complex doors with glass or carved panels, this stage takes the most time — and the most care.

04

Finishing

Every door receives a multi-step finishing process appropriate to its use — exterior or interior, painted or stained. Exterior doors get UV-resistant topcoats applied in multiple passes with sanding between coats. I don't rush this step.

05

Installation

Installation day is when everything comes together. I bring the door to your home and fit it to the opening — adjusting hinges, trimming if needed, setting the latch and strike, installing weather-stripping, and fitting the threshold. I don't leave until the door operates perfectly.

06

Aftercare

I provide care instructions specific to your door's finish and wood species, and I'm available if questions come up after installation. Exterior wood doors need periodic maintenance — I'll tell you exactly what to do and when, so your door stays in top condition for years.

California Compliance

Transparency on Licensing

Working Within California's Contractor Licensing Requirements

Master Door and Crown works in collaboration with licensed general contractors and specialty contractors where California law requires it, ensuring every project is completed safely, legally, and correctly. California's contractor licensing requirements vary based on project scope and total contract value. I am transparent about this with every client from the first conversation — you will always know exactly who is doing what on your project and why. My goal is a result that's built right, permitted correctly where required, and stands behind by everyone involved.
