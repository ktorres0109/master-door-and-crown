The Craftsman

My Name Is Roberto. I Build Doors.

I've been working with wood for over 20 years. Master Door and Crown is not a company — it's me, my shop, and the doors I build from scratch for homeowners throughout Ventura County.

The Story

Twenty Years, One Door at a Time

I started in construction out of necessity — framing, rough carpentry, whatever came through. But somewhere along the way I got put on a door crew, and that was it. There's something about a door: the way it has to be perfectly plumb, the way the grain has to run right, the way a good one feels when it swings. I never left that part of the trade.

When you call me, you get me — not a salesperson, not a project manager. I come to your house, look at the opening, look at the architecture, and we talk through what makes sense. I give you a clear number before any lumber is cut. I don't disappear on a project. This is a small operation by design, and that's exactly the point.

Philosophy

Why Wood. Why Custom. Why One Craftsman.

These aren't just talking points. They're the reasons every decision in this business is made the way it is.

01

Why Wood

The only material that gets better with age — and the only one you can actually repair when it needs attention.

02

Why Custom

Your opening has exact dimensions. A door pulled off a shelf doesn't know that — it gets shimmed and gapped and called done.

03

Why One Craftsman

When one person measures it, builds it, and hangs it, someone owns the outcome. That's how I run this business.

How It Works

The Build Process

From the first call to the last screw, here's how every project at Master Door and Crown unfolds.

01

Consultation

We talk through your project. I come to your home, take measurements, discuss design, and give you a clear number before any lumber is cut.

02

Fabrication

I select and mill the lumber in my shop, cut all joinery by hand, and assemble the door to your exact dimensions.

03

Finishing

Multi-step finishing appropriate to the door's use — UV-resistant topcoats for exterior, stain or paint for interior. No rushing.

04

Installation

I hang the door, set all hardware and weather-stripping, and don't leave until it operates perfectly.
