Why Custom Matters

Why We Don't Sell Doors Off a Truck

A big-box door is built for an opening that doesn't exist — a perfectly square one. Here's what changes when your door is built for your actual house.

Built to Your Opening

Older homes settle. Openings go out of square, jambs lean, floors slope. A pre-hung door gets shimmed and caulked until it more or less fits. A custom door is milled to the opening you actually have — so it closes with one finger and stays that way.

Wood Chosen for the Coast

Salt air and afternoon sun are hard on a front door. We pick the species and the finish for where the door will live — a west-facing entry in Oxnard gets different wood and a different topcoat than a shaded interior door in Ojai. That's the difference between refinishing in fifteen years or in three.

Curb Appeal That Holds Up

The entry is the first thing anyone sees up close — buyers included. A solid wood door, sized and styled for the house, reads as quality from the street and still does decades later. Hollow replacements look fine on day one and cheap by year five.

Made to Match the House

A Spanish colonial in Ojai, a mid-century ranch in Camarillo, and a new build in Thousand Oaks shouldn't get the same door. We match panel layout, sticking profile, and hardware to the architecture, so the door looks like it was always part of the house.
